April 2026 Situation Report
The pace of state AI legislation is accelerating despite federal preemption pressure — 43 states have active 2026 bills, nearly matching all of 2025. The White House AI Framework (March 20) has not slowed state action. Tennessee just became the latest state to enact healthcare AI law with a private right of action. Virginia's HB797 (IVO framework) was signed April 13. Colorado enforcement begins June 30 — a critical near-term deadline for any system with Colorado operations. Maryland's HB0820 remains the most immediate DMV-region binding obligation.
White House National AI Legislative Framework
Released March 20, 2026 · Policy document — not law
Policy only
Recommends Congress establish a federal AI standard preempting state laws deemed "unduly burdensome." 7 policy areas including child safety, innovation, and workforce. Has no binding force — Congress has twice rejected broad AI preemption (One Big Beautiful Bill; NDAA). As of April 19, the Commerce Dept. evaluation of "burdensome" state laws (due March 11) has still not been publicly released.
Two competing legislative vehicles: TRUMP AMERICA AI Act (Sen. Blackburn, ~300-page draft, March 18) and GUARDRAILS Act (Rep. Beyer, March 20) pull in opposite directions. Over 50 Republican state lawmakers across 24 states sent a letter to President Trump on March 3, 2026, urging the Administration to "discontinue efforts to block state AI laws." State laws remain fully enforceable — only Congress can preempt under the Supremacy Clause.

HB 797 / SB 384 — Independent AI Verification Organizations (IVO Framework)
Signed by Gov. Spanberger April 13, 2026 · Passed 84–14 / 40–0 · Effective July 1, 2026
Signed into law
Creates a framework for third-party Independent Verification Organizations (IVOs) — expert bodies that can verify whether AI systems adhere to safety and best practice standards. IVO applicants apply for licensure through VITA (Virginia Information Technologies Agency). Introduced by Del. Cliff Hayes (D-Chesapeake) and Sen. Angelia Williams Graves (D-Norfolk). Strong bipartisan passage signals consensus on AI governance infrastructure as a path forward.
Forward-looking infrastructure bill with no immediate clinical compliance burden for health systems. However, IVO certifications — once the framework is operational — could become a competitive differentiator: health systems that obtain IVO assessments of their AI tools can demonstrate independent governance rigor to regulators, payers, and patients. This mirrors the EU AI Act's conformity assessment approach. Health systems with robust AI governance programs should track IVO development for voluntary certification opportunities in 2027.

Tennessee SB1580 — AI Mental Health Professional Misrepresentation Prohibition
Signed by Gov. Bill Lee April 1, 2026 · Effective July 1, 2026 · Passed 32–0 / 94–0
Effective Jul 1, 2026
Prohibits any person from developing or deploying an AI system that advertises or represents to the public that such system is or is able to act as a qualified mental health professional. Violations are classified as violations of the Tennessee Consumer Protection Act — $5,000 civil penalty per violation plus a private right of action allowing affected individuals to sue for damages and injunctive relief. This is one of the first healthcare AI laws with a private right of action.
DMV health systems deploying behavioral health AI tools with any Tennessee patient population or operations must: review all marketing materials, product descriptions, and public representations for prohibited claims; remove any representations that AI can "act as" a mental health professional before July 1, 2026; assess AI systems deployed in Tennessee for compliance. The private right of action significantly elevates litigation risk compared to AG-only enforcement models. Template: this is the same type of law advancing in Virginia (SB269, deferred to 2027), Maryland (HB883), and now multiple other states.
Audit behavioral health AI marketing materials before July 1, 2026
